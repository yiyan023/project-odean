Defendant's Daubert motion directed to Plaintiff's productivity uplift expert, Prof. Hannah Ortiz, is GRANTED IN PART and DENIED IN PART.

Prof. Ortiz's headline opinion—that Plaintiff's CI/CD optimizations produced a "25% developer productivity gain across the industry"—rests on extrapolations far beyond the data. That portion of her testimony, which assumes widespread adoption and uniform effects unconstrained by the record, is excluded.

At the same time, Prof. Ortiz also offers a more modest calculation tied to actual usage logs and time-saved measurements within a subset of Plaintiff's customers. That narrower analysis employs a sufficiently reliable method—comparing pre- and post-deployment cycle times on matched projects—to assist the jury, and it will be permitted with appropriate limiting instructions.
